Identified Problem

In today’s digital age where computers are at the core of building efficiency yet, they pose a problem of ergonomics. Musculoskeletal disorders (MSDs) effect the nerve ,muscle, blood vessels, ligaments. According to Bureau of Statistics Labour (BSL) MSD cases accounted for 33% of all worker injury. While technology can be a boon it also can be prove to solve its own challenges. The main aim of our project is to eliminate MSD’s and improve posture. We discuss our approach in the following slides.

Ergonomics

Ergonomics is the adaptation of an environment (workspace, sports, leasure, health and safety) to best fit the people in that environment. Poor ergonomics lead to MSDs (Musculoskeletal Disorders) Some examples of MSDs: Carpal tunnel syndrome, Tendinitis, Rotator cuff injuries, Epicondylitis, Trigger finger, Muscle strains and low back injuries There are many different ways to prevent MSDs such as having ergonomic chairs and tables, but not many ways of detecting bad posture.

A motion capture approach

A six IR camera setup, using the Flex 13 and the Optihub 2 by OptiTrac, was used to create a 3D area in which IR markers can be used for motion capture. The software, “Motive” by OptiTrack, uses the Flex 13 cameras and IR markers to track and record data. Data from a singular camera is recorded in 2D space. Motive takes the data from multiple cameras then converts the 2D spaces into 3D coordinates.
